III · The Merged Experience

Case study and product fuse into one site. The page opens with the slider running on a real example. You scroll through chapters; each chapter reveals product moves inside the persistent scanner canvas. The cursor demos become real interactions you can take over. There is no separate "case study page" anymore — the strategic narrative is the product walkthrough.

Wow: "I've never seen a portfolio do this."
Ships: as one immersive page at rotemgotlieb.com/work/ghost, replaces the current case study.
Effort: 6-9 sprints. The highest novelty; the most invention.

My recommendation: III · The Merged Experience

Ghost's existing thesis — "let the slider be the wedge demo at Beat 2" — already proves the format works at chapter scale. III takes it to the whole page. I is wow-by-interactivity but the prose blocks still read as case-study scaffolding around a hot canvas — the canvas is a guest. II is the founder play but splits attention across two artifacts. III dissolves the case study format itself: the slider runs through every chapter, the deviation markers turn from demo callouts into real callouts you can poke, the AI Fix Flow plays at your finger when the narrative reaches the "remediation" beat. The reader doesn't read about the design-system reality scanner — they operate it while the strategic argument unfolds around their hands.

Risk: III demands inventing a portfolio format. Gated week-on-week sprints. If we hit a wall we can back down to I and still ship something great.
