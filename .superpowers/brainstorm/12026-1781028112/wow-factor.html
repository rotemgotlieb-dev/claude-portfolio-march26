III · The Merged Experience

Case study and product fuse into one site. You scroll/navigate through chapters; each chapter reveals product moves inside a single persistent Living Observatory canvas. The cursor demonstrations become real interactions you can take over. There is no separate "case study page" — the strategic narrative is the product onboarding.

Wow: "I've never seen a portfolio do this."
Ships: as one immersive page at rotemgotlieb.com/work/pulse, replaces the current case study.
Effort: 6-9 sprints. The highest novelty; the most invention.

My recommendation: III · The Merged Experience

Here's why. I is wow-by-interactivity but the case study scaffolding (sidebar, sections, prose blocks) still reads as a case study around a hot canvas — the canvas is a guest. II is the founder play but splits attention across two artifacts and the product carries the wow burden alone, which is a much larger surface to nail. III dissolves the case study format itself: the page is one canvas, the narrative is told through cursor-as-designer beats that hand off to the reader at each chapter break. That's the novelty hiring managers don't have words for yet, which is the actual definition of a standout portfolio piece. It also keeps shippability — the artifact is one URL.

The cost: III demands inventing a portfolio format, not just building a great case study. Three weeks of week-on-week sprints, gated. If we hit a wall at chapter 03 we can back down to I and still ship a great piece.

Pick one — or push back. "Even more ambition" / "actually let's stay closer to phased" are both valid responses.
