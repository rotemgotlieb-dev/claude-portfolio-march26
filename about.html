How I got here

I came to product design from Interdisciplinary Computing and the Arts at UC San Diego, a program that mixed code, visual design, sound, and interaction. It taught me that the most interesting design problems sit between disciplines, and that the best way to learn a new tool is to build something hard with it.

I finished my degree in December 2024 and started at LexisNexis Risk Solutions the same month, working on the ThreatMetrix fraud detection product. By the seventh month I'd built a Figma-to-Storybook token pipeline that took palette updates from a full day of three-person work down to thirty seconds. The thing I learned there that I keep using: design infrastructure compounds. A good token system doesn't just make designs consistent, it makes everyone on the team faster.

What I'm building now

Right now I'm a contract design engineer at Surface Labs, a YC company. I design pages in Claude and ship them to the production repo as reviewed pull requests; thirteen-plus are live. Before that I built Pulse and Ghost, two live AI products, alongside a VR golf game, a daily art practice, and my apparel brand.

Looking for a team to bring this pace into.
